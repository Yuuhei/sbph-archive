K's earphone nameless 1 week review: Disclaimer: I have started the audio hobby a few months ago (August 2020) So things i say may be incorrect or may not agree with your thoughts, so please take this review with a massive grain of salt. I bought these from fengru.ph in shopee for 139 php, these do not come with a microphone variant sadly. They do come with a free "soft" case. Build quality and Design: A lot better than those earphones that come from a phone. And the design is quite nice, with L and R indicators, with some branding. The advantage of the branding is that for example, you cannot see the L and R labels, you can use the branding as the indicator. How it works is you wear them so that the branding is facing outwards. Fit(May vary for others because all ears are different): They fit fine without foams, but for my ears, foams provide a better seal. Sound Quality(I did some a/b testing with and without foams. I ended up doing these tests with foams 9/10 of the time, since they fit better) : Bass: The kicks of drums are there, but they will not rattle your ears, so bassheads, look elsewhere. The bass guitar is more elevated here though. I don't know the proper terms, but i think i described the sub-bass and bass. Bass quality is good, they don't really drown out anything in the spectrum. Vocals: For those people who like listening to low volumes, the vocals are great here. There's only sibilance if you try pumping your volume higher. It has that nice airy sound, but can sound thin at times. Highs: Foams help with highs. When i tried them without the foams, the highs are extended but kind of thin sounding. When i put the foams, it improved the highs by cutting off the extension by a bit and it had more body or "fuller". (edited)
22:36
Soundstage: I think i can verify that buds are "wide soundstage on a budget." It was a breath of fresh air, since i was used to cheap earphones with only bass and closed soundstages for a lot of my life. Imaging: This is kind of where i had trouble, because i couldn't point exactly where are the instruments positioned, instead its more of a small area around a certain place. But to be honest that's asking too much for an earbud at this price. Comparison: QKZ VK4 - Bass and mids are better here, straight off the bat. The mids had better body than the nameless. But i cant blame the nameless, because it's probably the design or an earbud vs an IEM. The Nameless edges out in highs though. If i had these highs on the vk4, id probably not buy anything else. Xiaomi True Wireless earbuds 2 basic - The nameless smash them. If I describe the nameless as "wide sounstage done right", the xiaomi are "wide soundstage done bad", no depth. Everything is just spread on a plane. Final thoughts: 9/10 will recommend. Not for bassheads. But if you're on a tight budget, and prefer a bright set of earbuds? Put this on your list.
