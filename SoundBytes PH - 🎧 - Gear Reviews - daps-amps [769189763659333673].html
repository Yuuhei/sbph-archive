LG V10 "as DAP" Review
So it's been ilang weeks na rin sakin si LG V10 since nung na-repair ko siya (more into this later too) and it's been holding up since that day. Here are my observations of using the V10 as DAP: TL;DR: I'd prefer this over the other DAPs under 10k "as long as I know what I am doing". More into that later. BASIC INFORMATION/SPECS ABOUT THE V10: -Snapdragon 808 -32GB/64GB ROM (storage) -4GB RAM -3000MAH battery -16MP back camera, dual 5mp front (wide and normal lenses) -32-bit/192kHz audio (32-Bit DAC) -ESS SABRE 9018 DAC and 9602 Headphone Amplifier. (The specific V10 I am using: LG V10 F600s (Korean Variant), Stock Android Marshmallow 6.0.)
11:55
. Daily Usage: Performance: Everything is snappy and fluid since light- medium tasks lang naman ginagawa ko dito such as browsing via google, browsing twitter but most of the time pure audio listening lang talaga. I do sometimes use Youtube Music App whenever I want to discover new genres/ artists. Battery Life: Yes, this is the downfall of the V10 kahit nung bago pa siya. There's a battery drain (pero di malala). On Pure as DAP only usage, it lasted on me a day and a half (8 hours straight, then 4 hours the next day) While if naka-on na si WiFi siguro 3-4hours continous lang. But advantage nito is removable battery nya, something that most phones (and even some DAPs) can't do this present day. Sound: The most "important" for me sa phone na to. Basically anything na buds at iem ko na isalpak dito napapakanta nya. Maybe also headphones will sing here too, pero nakadepende siya sa sensitivity ng gear. The V10 can make any gears sing up to 600 Ohms (as stated sa settings). The sound of the V10 (tested with Jiushao e600 which is neutral sounding earbuds) is also neutral. I can even say na flat, with refined both ends ng spectrum. Everything is wide and clear with minimal to no noise can be heard. May nakita pa nga akong forum that they use the V10 as their reference DAP, which a good or a bad thing depende sa preference mo. (edited)
11:58
. NOW, TIME FOR THE ISSUES: Thermal throttling issues: Eto talaga pinakamain issue ng V10 or also the other phones na nakasnapdragon 800-821. Thermal throttling. Kaya di ko nirerecommend na gawin tong daily driver since medium -heavy use will fry this phone and baka Ikabootloop pa yun ng V10. Bootloop issue dito sa phone is not really sa software but sa hardware engineering ng phone (accdng to XDA). So far sa unit ko di pa naman nangyayari and wag naman sana. Also tried looking up if may issues specifically sa variant ng phone ko pero wala pa naman. Battery: Not really a big of a deal since this is removable pero yeah there's the battery drain issue. Still not big deal for me since I only use this most of the time as DAP. Image Retention (IR) Issue: When phone gets warm or a bit on the brightest setting for 5 minutes or so, lalabas image retention. This is like Amoled Burn pero sa IPS display. Avoiding bright colored backgrounds/images and turning it off if di naman need na naka on will return back to it's original state. Besides, di naman mata mo makikinig sa kanta.
11:59
FAQ: 1. May bnew pa nito? - Siguro meron pa, pero mahirap maghanap. 2nd hand units are available naman maghahanap lang. 2. Magkano 2nd Hand? - MSA Gadgets sells it for 3-5k depende sa condition. Issa good deal na. 3. Sinabi mo kanina "you prefer the V10 over some DAPs under 10k". why tho? - This sounds way better than other DAPs for its price range. I think this is one of the best DAPs you can buy to under 10K if you really want an Android DAP. Sa halagang 3k lang (accdng to some marketplace ads)naka android DAP ka na, ESS DAC pa. 4. Can this magically make my "brrt brrt" sounding gears sound like those summitfi/totl gears? - Synergy is still the key. Your Beats won't sound like a Beyerdynamic pag sinaksak sa V10. But hey, Vido's sound improved here. Also my Monk Espressos sounded different and very alive.I mean, mas maganda tunog ng monk if nasa proper source 5. Oh no! I fried up my board! ala na bootlooped na, gagana pa ba to? - Well worth a shot to look for some fix sa XDA but i wouldn't rely on that. Palit board is the key. 6. If I have the large amount of money to spend, should I still buy the V10? -Nope, go for the V20/30. or if trip mong bagong android DAP na pwede mong gawing daily driver, sagad mo na sa V60 . or kung rich boi ka, sagad mo na sa SP1K, nahiya ka pa e jk 7. Upgradeable pa ba to the latest Android? - I've seen some people na napagana nila to up to Nougat but I wouldn't risk it due to the issues stated.
12:00
. Verdict: The LG V10 still holds up to this present day! It is very good starting android DAP for newbies na gusto ng separate Android streaming DAP for Less. Di rin siya sensitive sa gears, very good for exploring the budgetfi-midfi hobby. As long as you know what you are doing, you'll be fine. I hope sa mga meron/ planning to grab the V10 sana ma-enjoy niyo yung audio experience tulad ko Sana this may help you to choose your budget DAP. Salamat PaPhi!

Walnut v3 Review! (dahil wala akong magawa)
Quick review lang ito, and this might answer some questions about this DAP Build: Mukang bomba more brick than a brick. Kasinlaki lang ng Walnut V2s. May tatlong headphone/earphone ports - dalawang 3.5mm ports (LO/PO), then isang 2.5mm port. Performance: As usual sa mga portable dap na kahawig nito (Walnut and Zishan), almost the same, may screen lang. As usual rin sa may mga screen na tulad nito, nagiging plantsa habang matagal mong ginagamit. Or it gets warm depende sa environment. Battery on paper is 10 hours pero max ko na dito is around ~5 hours. 2-3 hours to charge. UI: Finnicky ui neto pero pag nasanay ka madali lang siya gamitin naman. Need magpraktis para mapindot maayos ang tatskrin. Yung screen niya is same sa mga lumang phones dati, yung ginagamitan ng kuko . Same rin sila ng Zishan Z3 na may battery indicator na faulty. However, may charge indicator naman na ilaw si V3 sa baba when charging. (edited)
12:06
. Hardware: AK4490EQ. Etong nasa akin ay non-opamp rollable variant. Based sa mga nahanap kong info sa mga forums, ito daw yung pinakarecent version ng walnut v3. Kaya sila gumawa nito dahil daw mas maraming issues yung opamp rollable variant na diniscontinued rin nila. Firmware upgradeable rin and may download links parin sila sa forums. Sound: I’d say neutral with a bit of “softness” if that makes sense. Adequate lang body ng buong frequencies. Walang hint ng kahit ano. Pwede ko nga ata sabihing flat to e. Verdict: Worth bang bumili nito sa presyong brand new? Syempre hindi, mag FiiO M3K ka nalang or any other DAPs na nasa price range. (Probably this will be my last review sa mga ganito, temporary quit na rin. Might declutter some gears soon). Salamat sa pagbabasa, PaPhi!

Rebel Audio RebelAmp: The Half Kilobuck Resolving Green Goblin Amps that I’ve heard for reference: Magni 3+, Valhalla 2, Asgard 3, JDS Atom Sound: After a week of ownership, this amp still leaves me in awe with how much my 6XX scales with it. Improvement to other intangibles is present as well, the intimacy of my Sennheisers was expanded with more depth and better imaging through the Rebel. Asgard 3 was warmer when I demo'd it, but the Rebel is alot more detailed and provides better intangibles. Build: Very Schiit like design and build, but with more personality. The green is more muted IRL than in pictures so don’t be fooled, though the paint job feels nice to the touch. Super smooth volume pot and I appreciate that it's deep for better grab-ability, smaller switches in the front IO compared to Schiit’s but I don’t mind. Conclusion: I have heard this amp is majority based on AMB Lab’s M3 design but with a dual mono layout. Other amps in this price range include the Flux Labs FA-12s and Singxer SA-1, which both I have not heard. But what I can definitively say is that I regret nothing picking this amp over the Asgard 3, which I was on the fence about but ultimately decided on the $500 RebelAmp which is more than twice the price of the Asgard. It is worth every damn penny for the price premium. Source/s used: • Realme 5i • Samsung Galaxy S3 (Exynos variant) • Laptop (Compaq Presario CQ58), na naka Debian Stable. • PC, na naka Windows 10. • Any popular tracks na meron ako in any genre, ranging from MP3 320 to DSD 128. |

SPECS: • USB data port: Type-c • DSD sampling rate: DSD128 • PCM sampling rate: 384K 32Bit • Battery: 4200mah BUILD QUALITY,ACCESSORIES,PORTS,CONNECTIVITY BATTERY LIFE • The usual Zishan/ Walnut silhouette. Bakal na pwede ipukpok (pero wag nyo ipukpok syempre). • The usual rin na volume knob. Smooth sa umpisa. Pero sana di sya magkaroon ng crackling na tunog pag pinipihit pag tumagal na, tulad ng ibang products nila. However, meron namang lighter fluid na pwedeng ilagay. • Two separate usb-c ports, isang pang charge at isang pang DAC mode. • May LO at PO ports rin, even though useless yung LO port sa unit ko since wala nang separate amp mode si U1 unlike ng AKM variant. Flang V5 Thoughts and Impressions Note: This is just an impression of an average listener, so take this impression as a grain of salt. Gears Used (Most of my gears are already at my baggage so only these): • Moondrop Blessing 2 • Sony wh-1000xm3 • Sennheiser hd580 Precision Build Quality It is actually hefty, yet it doesn't look like structurally sound. It's too huge and thick to be portable. The scrolling wheel, while being a good additional gimmick, it sways (sloping, iygwim) and it feels like it can break to me. Clicky buttons aren't satisfying (for me). Buttons aren't that bad tho, It does feel that can last for a long time. The volume knob is also nice with, with abit of a hardness to slide it (well, to prevent accidents I guess?) OS, and Overall Feel While the OS does looks primitive, the navigation is easy, thanks to the scrolling wheel. It holds the very basic functions that is meant for this, music. While it does have a system update feature, files aren't existent, and there's no even update available so it doesn't really matter. And lastly, while not a big issue, it's annoying that it sometimes get the first second of the song skipped. Sound It actually sounded natural, except being having an emphasis in lower bass, which maybe enjoyable to use in a commute. The vocals doesn't sound distorted or anything. Treble doesn't sound harsh. It does have soundstage but doesn't sound like having a good separation (abit linear). This dap can drive the headphones well, yet it does have a floor noise at low volumes for iems. There is a knob for a bass, and turning it ON would make every frequencies being recessed and makes more emphasis in bass. Not a fan of it so I don't use it that much. Price and Verdict From what I seen, this is priced $300 before. I bought this one as second hand (unit only), for 5,500円 (around $42). While this is quite a good dap, it doesn't justify the old price tag. There are already alot of options out there, so I guess you can skip this one. On the plus side though (and also am a fan of it), the 6.35mm port included is actually a good one for me. So yeah if you are a basshead, you can consider this one. Otherwise, nope. That's it.
